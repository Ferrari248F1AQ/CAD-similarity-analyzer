How it works:
Measures how much the similarity criteria agree with each other (weighted by importance).
• Real plagiarism (all criteria high & coherent): similarity stays unchanged
• False positive (some high, some low): similarity is reduced
• Not plagiarism (all low & uniform): similarity stays unchanged

Key: Low-weight criteria (e.g. author 2%) barely affect the coherence score, so a plagiarist changing just the author name won't cause a false reduction.
0% = linear only 50% 100% = full coherence
At 0% fuzzy has no effect. At 100% discordant criteria are maximally reduced.
